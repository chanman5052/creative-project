The First Vision



Joseph Smith's Question


In the year 1820, a young farm boy by the name of Joseph Smith Jr. was also confused by all of the different churches where he lived. At this time the United States had undergone a second religious revival called The Second Great Awakening which left many people conflicted from the different teachings taught by all of the different churches. Young Joseph pondered how so many religions could preach so many different ideas from the same source, The Bible. Not only did he want to know which church was true but wanted to ensure that his soul could be saved by the answer. As he studied the scriptures he came across a scripture in the Bible which stated


If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him. (James 1: 5-6)
